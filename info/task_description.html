Learning barcode specifications and implementing barcode reader with Python.

This time I will use EAN-13 (from Wikipedia).

The input representing the image of the barcode is a String of length 95. Each digit is a underscore or space. A underscore means bar area. You should decode the barcode and make the output is a String of length 13.

In the case of a barcode which does not conform to the specification, return None.

Example:

barcode_reader(
    '_ _   _ __ _  ___ __  __  _  __ ____ _  ___ _ _ _ __  __ __ __  _    _ _ ___  _  ___ _   _  _ _') == '5901234123457'

barcode_reader(
    '_ _   __ _ _  ___ ____ _   _ __  _ ___  ___ _ _ _ __  __ _  ___ _  ___ _ ___  _  _   _ _    _ _') == '4003994155486'

Input: The image of the barcode is a String of length 95. It consists only of underscore or space.

Output: The decoded barcode number is a String of length 13.

How it is used:
understand the mechanism of barcode.

Precondition:

  • len(barcode) == 95
  • all(b in ('_', ' ') for b in barcode)
